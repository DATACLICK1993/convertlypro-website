Passport Photo Requirements 2026: Size, Background & Rules by Country

📅 March 2026⏱️ 7 min read📂 Reference👤 ConvertlyPro Team

Passport photo rejections delay your application by weeks. This complete guide covers exact requirements for India, USA, UK and other countries — so your photo is accepted the first time.

Universal Passport Photo Rules

Regardless of country, virtually all passport photo requirements share these common elements:

  • White or off-white background: The background must be plain, light, and free of patterns or shadows. Light grey is accepted by most countries. Blue backgrounds are only used for some visa types.
  • Face must be centered and fill 70–80% of the frame: Your face should be prominent. The crown of your head to your chin should occupy most of the photo height.
  • Eyes must be open and clearly visible: No hair covering eyes, no heavy shadows across the face.
  • Neutral expression or natural smile: Most countries now allow a natural smile. Extreme expressions are always rejected.
  • No glasses: Since 2013, most countries (including India, USA, UK) no longer allow glasses in passport photos.
  • Recent photo: Usually taken within the last 6 months. Some countries specify within the last 3 months.

India Passport Photo Requirements

Size: 2 inch × 2 inch (51 mm × 51 mm)
Background: Plain white
Face coverage: 70–80% of photo height
File size (digital): 20 KB to 1 MB
Format: JPEG only
Resolution: Minimum 600×600 pixels, maximum 1200×1200 pixels

Indian passport photos are among the most standardized. The photo must be taken on a white background — not cream or off-white. The applicant must look directly at the camera with both eyes open. Photographs with shadows on the face or background are commonly rejected.

For Tatkal applications, extra sets of photos are often required. Keep digital copies at exactly the specified dimensions.

USA Passport Photo Requirements

Size: 2 inch × 2 inch (51 mm × 51 mm)
Background: Plain white or off-white
Face coverage: 1 inch to 1⅜ inches from chin to top of head
File size (digital): Between 240×240 and 1200×1200 pixels, under 240 KB
Format: JPEG
Head position: Directly facing camera, no head tilt

The US State Department is strict about head tilt. Even a slight tilt is grounds for rejection. The face must be square to the camera. Off-white and white backgrounds are both acceptable — a flat white works best.

For digital submissions (passport renewal online), the file must be between 240×240 and 1200×1200 pixels and under 240 KB — which often requires compression. Use ConvertlyPro's size compressor to hit the exact requirement.

UK Passport Photo Requirements

Size: 35 mm × 45 mm (standard UK size — different from USA)
Background: Cream or light grey (NOT pure white)
Face coverage: 29–34 mm from chin to crown
File size (digital): Between 50 KB and 10 MB
Format: JPEG or PNG

Note: The UK uses a different size than the USA (35×45 mm vs 51×51 mm). This is a common mistake. The background specification is also different — the UK specifically states "cream or light grey" background, NOT pure white. Using a pure white background may cause rejection.

Canada Passport Photo Requirements

Size: 50 mm × 70 mm (different from USA and UK)
Background: Plain white
Face coverage: 31–36 mm from chin to crown
Format: Printed photo required (digital not accepted for primary applications)

Canada's passport photo size is unique at 50×70 mm — larger than US and UK. Digital submissions are not accepted for new Canadian passports. The photo must be printed by a professional photo service.

Common Passport Photo Rejection Reasons

Immigration authorities reject passport photos for these most common reasons:

  1. Shadow on face or background — Caused by poor lighting or standing too close to the backdrop. Use diffused lighting from two sides to eliminate shadows.
  2. Wrong background color — Using light blue instead of white, or white instead of cream (for UK applications).
  3. Glasses — No longer accepted by most countries since 2013.
  4. Head covering — Religious head coverings may be allowed if your face from forehead to chin is fully visible. Hats and caps are never allowed.
  5. Wrong dimensions — Printing at the wrong size or aspect ratio.
  6. Photo too old — Most countries require photos taken within the last 6 months.
  7. Digital manipulation — Retouching or filters that alter appearance.
  8. File size out of range — Digital submissions that are too large or too small.

How to Take a Passport Photo at Home

Professional passport photo studios charge ₹100–₹300 per set in India, or $15–$20 in the USA. You can take a perfectly acceptable photo at home:

  1. Background: Tape a white sheet of paper or white cloth flat against a wall. Ensure no wrinkles or shadows.
  2. Lighting: Use natural daylight from a window in front of you. Avoid direct sunlight which creates harsh shadows. Position one lamp on each side to eliminate shadows.
  3. Camera: A smartphone at portrait mode works well. Have someone take the photo — selfies have a slight distortion from the short focal length.
  4. Distance: Stand about 2 meters from the camera. Include your shoulders in the frame — you'll crop to face later.

Printing Passport Photos

After resizing digitally, you'll need to print your photos. Standard approach:

  • Place 4–6 copies of your resized photo on a 4×6 inch (10×15 cm) template in Photoshop, Canva, or Google Slides
  • Print at a photo printing service (Walmart, CVS, local photo shops)
  • Cut with scissors along the edges

This typically costs ₹15–₹30 for a 4×6 print in India, or $0.25–$0.50 in the USA — far cheaper than getting the photo taken at a studio.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I wear jewelry in a passport photo?
A: Yes, simple jewelry is generally allowed. Avoid anything that obscures your face or creates reflections under flash photography.

Q: Is smiling allowed in passport photos?
A: Most countries now allow a natural, closed-mouth smile. A wide open-mouth smile is typically rejected. A neutral expression is always safest.

Q: How recent does a passport photo need to be?
A: India and USA require photos taken within 6 months. UK requires within the last month for renewal.
